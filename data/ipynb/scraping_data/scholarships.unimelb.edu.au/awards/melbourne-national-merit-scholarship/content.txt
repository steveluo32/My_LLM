Summary
This scholarship is awarded to talented students from states and territories of Australia other than Victoria to assist with the cost of moving to Melbourne.





Benefits
A one-off allowance of $8,000 paid in the first semester of enrolment.Accommodation Close to CampusIf you live in rural or regional Victoria or outside Victoria, then you are eligible for priority placement in the Melbourne Accommodation Program. To support your transition to Melbourne, the University can accommodate you in one of our residential halls or partner-provided facilities close to the Parkville Campus. The offer does not include the cost of the accommodation. Places are limited so apply early to avoid disappointment.To register and for more details, refer to University Accommodation.

Eligibility and selection criteria
Eligibility
To be considered for this scholarship, you must:be an Australian citizen or permanent resident or a New Zealand citizenhave completed Australian Year Twelve or International Baccalaureate in a state or territory of Australia other than Victoriahave applied for a University of Melbourne undergraduate course via VTAC for commencement in the year following completion of Australian Year Twelve or International Baccalaureatesatisfy the prerequisites for a University of Melbourne undergraduate course and have achieved an ATAR of 99.00 – 99.85not have previously undertaken any tertiary studies (excluding extension studies completed as part of a Year 12 program)
Selection criteria
This scholarship is guaranteed to all eligible students.
Conditions
This scholarship is subject to the Coursework Scholarships Terms & Conditions and can be deferred to the following academic year. Also you cannot hold both a Melbourne National Merit Scholarship and a Melbourne Chancellor’s Scholarship.

Application
How to apply
If you have applied for admission to the University via VTAC, you will automatically be considered for the Melbourne National Merit Scholarship.
When will I know the outcome?
Offers are made in December following the release of ATAR results.
