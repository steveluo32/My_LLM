The School of Computing and Information Systems (CIS) and the Department of Infrastructure Engineering (IE) are calling for applications for the 2023 Research Collaboration Seed Funding scheme.This seed funding initiative aims to encourage research collaborations across the multiple disciplines in CIS and IE, connecting researchers from the two divisions on projects that will lead to future externally-funded research applications.The initiative will support cross-School/Department research projects up to $30,000.Goals of the Seed Funding InitiativeTo nurture new collaborations, leading to long-term research connections between CIS and IE.To build new research capacity, particularly with a view to garnering external research funding.To advance innovative research ideas, bringing together the expertise of CIS and ME researchers.We are calling for proposals that meet the above goals, show significant potential for future funding submissions, and have potential for broad impact.Assessment criteriaRequirements:The project must include a Chief Investigator from both CIS and IE. The aim is to foster collaboration between School and Department.The project must be new.The project must identify and target a significant external funding opportunity (e.g., ARC Linkage or DP, industry contract, etc.).Additional considerations that will be used by the selection panel in ranking and selecting submissions include:Why seed funding is essential to the success of this project.The plan for this project to lead into a submission of a larger funding bid.The novelty of the project and collaboration, in relation to the people, disciplines and School/Department involved.The inclusion of ECRs.Potential for the project to address the UN's Sustainable Development Goals.See Seed Funding Guidelines for full details.FundingMaximum funding amount is $30,000.Application ProcessRead the CIS-IE Seed Funding GuidelinesCheck the Key Dates belowLooking for a co-CI?
